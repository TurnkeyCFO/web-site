The general contractor reality

Why a real website wins for GCs specifically.

Large remodels involve a long sales cycle, significant financial risk, and a homeowner who will research you thoroughly. Your website has to perform across all three dimensions — not just look decent.

The research phase is long and real

Homeowners spend weeks before calling

A homeowner planning a $75,000 addition doesn't call the first name they find. They visit your website multiple times, compare your portfolio to competitors, and build a case for who they trust. A website that performs across that multi-week research journey is what converts them — not a one-page flyer.

Credibility is the entire sale

The homeowner is handing you their most valuable asset

They're opening their home, living in a construction zone, and writing large checks over months. Your licensing, your process, your project history, and your professional presentation aren't extras — they're the reason they choose you over the contractor who gave a lower bid.

Portfolio proof reduces decision friction

They need to see it before they can commit

Homeowners can't visualize the finished project from a description — they need to see it. A deep project gallery organized by type (kitchens, bathrooms, additions) lets them find their exact project and see your quality firsthand, which is often what moves them from "thinking about it" to "let's talk."

Financing unlocks the "almost ready" segment

Many homeowners are closer than they think

A significant share of homeowners want the remodel but believe they can't afford it right now. A website that clearly presents financing options — and demystifies the payment timeline — converts those "not yet" visitors into qualified consultations your competitors never even talk to.

General contractor FAQ

Questions contractors ask us first.

I get most of my GC work through referrals and repeat clients. Do I really need a website?

Referrals are your best leads — but those leads Google you before they ever pick up the phone. For a $30,000 kitchen remodel or a $150,000 addition, homeowners do serious research. A website with your project gallery, licensing, and transparent process converts that warm referral into a scheduled consultation instead of a missed opportunity.

How does a website help with big-ticket, long-sales-cycle projects?

A homeowner planning a major remodel will visit your website multiple times over weeks or months before contacting you. A strong site with detailed project photos, a clear process walkthrough, and transparent licensing keeps you top of mind and builds trust throughout that research period — so when they're ready to move forward, you're the obvious call.

What should a general contractor website show to win more bids?

The three things that move the needle: a project gallery with real before-and-after photos organized by project type, visible licensing and insurance credentials, and a clear step-by-step process that reduces the fear of "what will this be like to live through." Financing options and real client reviews round it out.

Will my general contractor website show up on Google?

Every site we build ships SEO-ready: proper title tags, local-business schema, service-specific pages, and fast mobile load. Paired with your Google Business Profile, you have a real shot at ranking for "general contractor near me," "kitchen remodel [your city]," and "home addition Texas."
