The First Visit

Nobody cries on the way in. Or on the way out.

Most of the fear around a dental visit comes from not knowing what happens next. So here is exactly what a first appointment looks like, start to finish, before you ever walk in.

01

You arrive, they relax

Kids come back to a room built at their scale, with TVs on the ceiling above every station. Parents come back too. There is no separating a nervous child from you.

02

A gentle cleaning

A hygienist counts, brushes and polishes, narrating every step out loud so nothing is a surprise. Distraction technique is a real skill here, and the team has it down.

03

Digital x-rays, if needed

Digital sensors mean dramatically reduced radiation compared with film, and images appear on screen immediately so you can see what we see.

04

The doctor exam

Dr. Jodie or Dr. Glynn checks growth, bite, spacing and every surface, then walks you through what they found in plain language, not dental shorthand.

05

A plan you agree to

If treatment is needed, you hear the options, the timing and the reasoning first. If a referral serves your child better than we can, we tell you that too.

Our Doctors

Two board-certified pediatric dentists. Both served first.

Dr. Jodie and Dr. Glynn Spencer are both Diplomates of the American Board of Pediatric Dentistry. Before either of them treated a child, each of them wore a uniform.

U.S. Navy · Full Scholarship

Dr. Jodie Spencer

Board-Certified Pediatric Dentist

Born and raised in Maryville, Missouri. She attended dental school at UMKC on a full Navy scholarship and was commissioned in 1999, then served three years stationed in Japan, where her two youngest children were born. She was recognized as Junior Officer of the Quarter and of the Year, and earned two Navy and Marine Corps Achievement Medals and a Navy Commendation Medal.

Her patients get what the practice calls a gentle mother's touch. Away from the office she is usually camping with family, on the boat, traveling or reading.

U.S. Army · Combat Infantryman

Dr. Glynn Spencer

Board-Certified Pediatric Dentist

He grew up in Springdale, Arkansas as an avid outdoorsman. After a year of college he enlisted in the U.S. Army and served as a combat infantryman at Ft. Benning and Ft. Riley. He later studied at Washburn University and graduated with Honors in Pediatric Dentistry from UMKC, receiving the Arthur Iwersen Award for Excellence in Pediatric Dentistry.

Outside the practice he restores vehicles, especially Jeeps, and spends his time on football, boating, hiking, hunting, fishing and family.
