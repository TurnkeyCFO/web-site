Concrete contractor FAQ

Questions concrete contractors ask us first.

I get most of my concrete work through referrals — do I really need a website?

Referrals are valuable, but every referral searches your name online before they call. With no website or a weak one, that warm lead second-guesses you and may never dial. A professional site validates your work, shows your project gallery, and captures the homeowners and property owners searching "concrete contractor near me" on their own — work that referrals alone never reach.

Concrete is a big-ticket job. How does a website help close the deal?

At the price point of a new driveway, patio, or foundation, homeowners want proof before they commit. A website with real project photos, clear service descriptions, customer reviews, and a professional look gives them that proof. It's what separates the contractor who gets called for an estimate from the one who gets passed over.

What if I already have a Facebook page or Nextdoor presence?

Social platforms help, but you don't own them. Algorithm changes or account issues can make your presence disappear. Your own website is an asset you fully control — and it's the first thing Google surfaces when someone searches for a concrete contractor in your service area, not your Facebook posts.

Will my concrete contractor website actually rank on Google?

Every site we build ships SEO-ready: proper title tags, local-business schema, service-area pages, and fast mobile load — the technical foundations Google rewards. Combined with your Google Business Profile, you have a real shot at showing up when property owners search for concrete work in your city.
