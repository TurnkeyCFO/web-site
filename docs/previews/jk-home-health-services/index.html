Care Programs

Six programs, one personalized plan.

No two conditions are the same, so no two care plans are either. Every plan starts with a full health assessment and is built around what you actually need.

Chronic Disease Care

A control plan built to help you manage long-term illness, stay independent and reduce hospital visits. Includes Alzheimer's disease, dementia, Parkinson's disease and congestive heart failure.

Neurological Injury

Comprehensive rehabilitation after a stroke, anoxia, traumatic brain injury or other neurological event. The goal is to enhance function, lessen symptoms and restore wellbeing.

Cardiopulmonary Rehab

A medically supervised program of exercise and education tailored to each individual, for people diagnosed with heart or lung disease. Built to boost endurance and quality of life.

Personal Care

Help with the everyday things: bathing, dressing and grooming, basic housekeeping, medication reminders. Delivered by care providers who are genuinely good at being in someone's home.

Orthopedic Rehabilitation

A physician-supervised program for recovery from musculoskeletal disease, injury or surgery, correcting limitations and relieving pain from trauma, illness or an operation.

Medical Home Care

Physician-prescribed home health delivered by a nurse, nurse assistant, home health aide or doctor. These are the services you would ordinarily receive in a hospital, rehab facility or nursing home.

Not sure which fits? Call 972-329-3900 and we will talk it through.

Getting Started

The first 24 hours.

Starting home health should not feel like paperwork. Here is exactly what happens from the moment a referral reaches our office.

01

The referral arrives

Your physician sends a referral, or you call us directly for non-medical care. Our intake form can be downloaded and faxed to 972-329-3903 at any hour.

02

We call you back within 24 hours

A real person from our office reaches out. We confirm the referral, review your insurance and answer the questions families always have before anyone sets foot in the house.

03

An in-home assessment

We come to your home and assess the real conditions: the stairs, the medications, the routine, the person. Not a checklist filled out over the phone.

04

Your care plan begins

We recommend the plan and the services, coordinate directly with your healthcare provider, and start care. You will know what to expect before it starts.

Common Questions

Straight answers, first.

How do we start home health services?

Your provider makes a referral for services. Download the intake form from our office, or call us at 972-329-3900 and we will walk you through it. Once we have it, we come to your home to do an assessment and recommend the care plan and services you need.

Do I need a referral from my provider?

It depends on the service. Non-medical home care needs no pre-authorization or prescription, so call us directly. Medical home care does need your doctor's authorization, because the care plan is written from the physician's orders.

Do you take Medicare and Medicaid?

We are a Medicare-certified agency and we are listed in the Superior HealthPlan and Amerigroup Texas Medicaid managed care directories. Depending on medical necessity, your insurance may or may not cover your care. We review your coverage up front and tell you about any out-of-pocket expense before care begins.

How much will services cost?

No two care plans are the same, so there is no single price. Cost depends on the service and the health condition behind it. Call us and we will set up a conversation about the care plan you want and what the service fees would be.

How qualified is your staff?

Every staff member goes through a screening process and an interview before selection, and we verify their background and licensing. Our team includes nurses, nurse assistants, home health aides and therapists.

Are you available 24/7?

Yes. We offer around-the-clock service, and a professional manager is available to answer your questions and offer clinical help.
