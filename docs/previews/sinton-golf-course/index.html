Our Story

A hometown course, worth the drive.

Since 1968, Sinton Golf Course has been the kind of place where you show up, drop your ball in the spindle, and play when it drops to the bottom.

Set inside Rob & Bessie Welder Park just north of town on Highway 181, the course rolls across open coastal-plain fairways stitched with mature live oaks and mesquite. There are no bunkers to hide behind out here — the wind off the gulf is the defense, and it keeps even scratch players honest.

Under a new board of directors, the course is in the middle of a real renovation: fresh turf, new carts, and renewed greens. It's a friendly, unhurried muni that plays like a private track and welcomes everyone from first-timers to lifelong members.

The Course

Eighteen honest holes,
shaped by the wind.

Par 72 across three tee sets. Small, true greens. Wide, forgiving fairways lined with oaks. Every par 5 from the middle tees plays under 500 yards — but let that coastal breeze come up, and the whole course changes character.
