The Craft

Four steps between a rack and a garment that fits.

Nothing here is guessed. Every piece follows the same disciplined line — the one seam that separates a real tailor from a hem-and-hope.

01

Measure

A proper set of measurements taken in person — shoulders, sleeve, seat, rise. The number the tape gives, not the one the size chart guesses.

02

Cut & mark

Chalk, pins, and patience. The cloth is opened and marked to your lines — the point of no return, done with a steady hand and years of feel for the fabric.

03

Fit

You come back, you put it on, and it gets honest feedback. Nipped here, let out there — the fitting is where "close enough" becomes exactly right.

04

Finish

Pressed, hand-finished, and pressed again. Often ready in about two days — because a garment you can't wear yet isn't finished, it's waiting.
