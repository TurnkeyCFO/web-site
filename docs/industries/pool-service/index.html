Word of mouth is great — but the first thing that referral does is Google your company name before they sign up for a service plan. A missing or weak site loses that warm lead fast. A real website confirms you're reliable, shows your service plans clearly, and captures the pool owners actively searching who no referral will ever reach.

What about Angi or Thumbtack for pool service leads?

Those platforms sell the same lead to multiple competitors, and the fees compound every month. Your own website is a permanent asset — it builds your brand, earns direct service-plan signups, and the cost stays fixed at $50/mo no matter how many recurring clients it generates.

I post before/after pool photos on Facebook. Isn't that enough?

A homeowner looking for weekly pool cleaning searches Google, not Facebook. Facebook pages rarely surface for "pool service near me," can't be optimized for local search, and give you no way to capture a maintenance plan inquiry at midnight when the homeowner is watching TV and thinking about their green pool.
