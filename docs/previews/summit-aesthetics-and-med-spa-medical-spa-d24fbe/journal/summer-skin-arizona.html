Seasonal

Protecting your skin through an Arizona summer.

4 min read By the Summit Clinical Team The Summit Journal

A Phoenix summer is not a gentle season for skin. Months of intense UV, dry desert air, and the constant swing between heat outdoors and air conditioning indoors all leave a mark. The good news: a few deliberate changes carry your skin through it — and a couple of treatments are actually better saved for the cooler months.

Sunscreen stops being optional

In Arizona, daily broad-spectrum SPF is the single most important thing you can do for your skin — not for a beach day, but for the drive to work and the walk across a parking lot. UV exposure is the leading driver of visible ageing, and the Valley delivers it generously.

  • Use a broad-spectrum SPF 30 or higher, every single morning.
  • Reapply through the day if you're outdoors — one morning application is not enough.
  • A tinted mineral formula doubles as light coverage and is easy to reapply over makeup.
  • Don't forget the often-missed zones: ears, neck, hands, and décolletage.

Hydrate for the desert, not the coast

Desert air pulls moisture from the skin barrier. If your skin feels tight, looks dull, or is suddenly more reactive, a compromised barrier is often why. Layer a hydrating serum — hyaluronic acid is a workhorse here — under a moisturiser, and don't strip your skin with harsh cleansers.

The summer goal is simple: protect and hydrate now, so the cooler months are free for the treatments that need them.

Time your treatments with the season

Some treatments pair badly with intense sun. Several lasers and deeper resurfacing peels leave skin temporarily more sun-sensitive, so they are best planned for fall and winter, when sun exposure is easier to control. Summer is a better window for:

  • HydraFacials — hydrating, no downtime, and an ideal monthly reset.
  • Injectables — tox and filler are not sun-restricted in the same way.
  • Lighter peels — with diligent sun protection afterwards.
  • IV hydration — a genuine help after time in the heat.

If a deeper laser series is on your list, the smart move is to consult now and schedule it for the cooler months. Your clinician will map the timing with you.

A simple summer routine

You don't need to overhaul everything — just protect more and strip less:

  • AM — gentle cleanse, antioxidant serum, hydrator, broad-spectrum SPF.
  • PM — cleanse, hydrating serum, moisturiser; ease back slightly on strong actives if your skin is reactive.
  • Reapply SPF whenever you're out, and keep a tinted mineral SPF in your bag.

The bottom line

A Valley summer rewards a protect-and-hydrate strategy and patience with the treatments that prefer cooler weather. Get the timing right and you head into fall with a healthy barrier — and the perfect window for the treatments you've been planning. A seasonal consultation is the easiest way to build that calendar.

A note on this article. This is general seasonal guidance, not personal medical advice. Treatment timing should be planned with a clinician who knows your skin and history.
