Injectables

A first-timer's honest guide to injectables.

6 min read Reviewed by Dr. Chen, Medical Director The Summit Journal

If you've been curious about Botox or filler but quietly nervous to actually book, you are in good company. Most of our first-time clients arrive with the same two worries: that it will hurt, and that they'll walk out looking like someone else. Here is the honest version of what to expect — and how to choose a clinic you can trust with your face.

It starts with a conversation, not a needle

A good first appointment is mostly talking. At Summit, your injector spends the first part of the visit understanding what's actually bothering you. Often a client points at a line and says “fix this” — and the real answer is something else entirely. That conversation is the treatment's most important step.

You should never feel rushed, and you should never feel sold to. If a clinic moves you straight to a chair, that's a sign to slow down.

The difference between tox and filler

These two get lumped together, but they do opposite jobs:

  • Neuromodulators (Botox, Dysport) relax the small muscles that create movement lines — frown lines, forehead lines, crow's feet. They soften expression without adding volume.
  • Dermal fillers are gels, usually hyaluronic acid, that restore volume that's been lost — cheeks, lips, under-eyes, jawline. They add structure rather than relax muscle.

Many first-timers only need one, not both. A trustworthy injector will tell you if that's the case.

Does it hurt?

Honestly: less than most people expect. Neuromodulator injections feel like a quick pinch and are over in minutes. Filler is a little more involved, but the products contain a numbing agent, and topical numbing is available for sensitive areas like the lips. The discomfort is brief and very manageable.

The goal is to look rested, not different. If anyone can tell you've had work done, the work wasn't subtle enough.

How to choose a great injector

This is the part that matters most. Aesthetic injecting is a medical procedure, and the person holding the syringe should be properly trained and supervised. Before you book anywhere, ask:

  • Is the practice physician-supervised, and who is the medical director?
  • What are the injector's credentials and training?
  • Will I get a follow-up appointment to review my results?
  • Does the clinic ever recommend against a treatment?

That last question is the quiet giveaway. The best injectors are comfortable saying “you don't need that.” At Summit, our injectors are genuinely known for talking clients out of treatments — and it's one of the things our clients trust us for most.

After your treatment

Most clients return to their day immediately. With filler, minor swelling or a small bruise can appear and usually settles within a few days. Neuromodulator results build gradually over five to ten days — so don't judge the result the next morning. We schedule a two-week follow-up with every injectable visit precisely so we can review and fine-tune.

The bottom line

A first injectable appointment should feel calm, unhurried, and honest. You should leave understanding exactly what was done and why. If that's not how it feels, you're in the wrong chair — and there's no shame in walking away to find the right one.

When you're ready, a Summit consultation is a no-pressure place to start the conversation.
