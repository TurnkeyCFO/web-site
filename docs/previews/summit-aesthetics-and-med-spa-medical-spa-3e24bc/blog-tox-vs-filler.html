Tox vs. Filler: What Each One Actually Does

Treatment Guide · 6 min read

“Tox” and “filler” get mentioned in the same breath constantly — but they do almost opposite things. Knowing the difference is the first step to a result that looks natural.

The one-line difference

A wrinkle relaxer (Botox, Dysport — what people loosely call ‘tox’) relaxes a muscle. A dermal filler adds volume. One quiets movement; the other restores structure. Most people who want to look refreshed eventually use a little of both — but for different lines, in different places.

What wrinkle relaxers do

Tox treats dynamic lines — the creases that appear when you move. Frown lines between the brows, forehead lines and crow’s feet are the classic three. By gently relaxing the muscle underneath, the skin above it stops folding as sharply, and the line softens.

  • Best for: lines that appear when you expression-make
  • Common areas: forehead, between the brows, around the eyes
  • Onset: 3 to 7 days, full effect around two weeks
  • Lasts: roughly 3 to 4 months

It can also do quieter, clever things — a subtle brow lift, a softer jawline, relief from jaw clenching and TMJ tension. A skilled injector treats the muscle pattern, not just the wrinkle.

What dermal fillers do

Filler treats static lines and lost volume — the changes that are visible even when your face is completely still. As we age, we lose fat and bone support, especially in the cheeks, temples and around the mouth. Filler restores that scaffolding.

  • Best for: volume loss, deep folds, contour, lip definition
  • Common areas: cheeks, under-eye, lips, smile lines, jawline
  • Onset: immediate, settling over one to two weeks
  • Lasts: roughly 6 to 18 months depending on product and area
If a line is there when your face is at rest, it usually wants filler. If it only shows up when you move, it usually wants tox.

Why ‘natural’ is a skill, not a product

Overdone work almost always comes from treating the wrong thing, or too much of it — not from the product itself. Filler chasing a dynamic line. Tox freezing a face that needed support, not stillness. The fix is rarely ‘less injectable’ in general; it is the right injectable, in the right plane, by someone who reads the whole face.

This is the entire reason Summit injectables are performed only by master-certified injectors. Anatomy-first training is what keeps a result subtle — and consistent from one visit, and one location, to the next.

How to decide

You do not have to. That is what a complimentary consultation is for. We will look at your face in motion and at rest, ask what bothers you (not a trend), and build a plan — which may well be ‘start with a little tox and reassess in a month.’ Honest beats aggressive, every time.

A note on this article. This guide is educational and reviewed for general accuracy under physician supervision. It is not medical advice for your specific situation — that always begins with a consultation.
