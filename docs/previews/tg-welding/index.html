The Work Few Shops Will Take

Water gaps that
move with the river

Where a creek or river crosses a fence line, the fence has to give when the water rises and settle back when it drops. We build water gaps on steel panels and hinges that swing with the flow, then hold the line again once it passes. It is intricate, patient work, and it is the kind of job that built our name out here.

Our Story

From a Georgetown shop to the green building on Highway 29

TG Welding started in Georgetown under owner and operator Tim Giebelhaus. As the work grew, so did where it came from. About half the jobs were already running out of Liberty Hill, Bertram, Georgetown and the Highway 29 corridor toward Austin, so the shop moved to meet them.

Today the crew works out of the recognizable green building on West Highway 29, just down from Liberty Hill High School. It is a small, hands-on team that has grown fast on one thing: doing honest work, finishing it on time, and standing behind it.
