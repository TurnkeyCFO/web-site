What goes wrong

Six ways a tire ruins your day. One number fixes all of them.

You should not have to limp a car across town on a donut, or leave it in a parking lot overnight. Tell Mike where you are and what the car is. He loads the right tire and drives to it.

Flat on the shoulder

Pull over safely, stay in the car, send your location. Mike comes to the roadside and changes it there.

Blowout, no spare

No spare in the trunk is not a dead end. He carries new and used tires in common sizes and fits one on the spot.

The slow leak you keep airing up

Stop feeding quarters to the air machine. A proper plug or patch, done right, and the tire holds.

A nail in the tread

If it can be repaired, he repairs it instead of selling you a tire. If it can't, he tells you straight.

2 a.m. in a parking lot

Open 24 hours means 24 hours. Late shift, early flight, holiday weekend, the phone still gets picked up.

Nobody has your size

Give him the numbers off the sidewall when you call. He shows up with the correct size, not a close-enough one.
