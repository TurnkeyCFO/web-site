Beginner's Strength Routine — 4 Week Plan

A progressive strength plan for women new to lifting or returning after a long break. Three sessions per week, 30-40 minutes each. Compound movements only — the lifts that actually move the needle.

Equipment: A pair of adjustable dumbbells (or a couple of fixed pairs), a sturdy bench or chair, and floor space. That's it.

The 5 Movements You'll Master

  1. Goblet Squat — hold one dumbbell at chest, squat to parallel.
  2. Romanian Deadlift (RDL) — hinge at hips, dumbbells slide down thighs.
  3. Dumbbell Row — one hand on bench, row dumbbell to ribs.
  4. Overhead Press — dumbbells from shoulders to overhead.
  5. Glute Bridge — on floor, drive heels down, squeeze glutes hard.

Week 1 & 2 — Foundation

Sessions: Mon / Wed / Fri (or any 3 non-consecutive days).

Sets x Reps: 3 x 8-10 per movement. Rest 60-90 seconds between sets.

Week 3 & 4 — Build

Sets x Reps: 4 x 8 per movement. Add 5 lbs when you complete all reps with good form.

Progression Rules

  • Form first. If form breaks, the set is over. Quality > reps.
  • Add weight slowly. 2.5-5 lbs per movement per week is plenty.
  • Last rep should feel hard. If reps 8-10 are easy, the weight is too light.
  • Track every session. Notebook, phone, app — pick one and stick to it.

What Changes in 4 Weeks

Week 1

Movement quality. You learn the lifts.

Week 2

Confidence. You stop second-guessing form.

Week 3

Strength. Weights start climbing.

Week 4

Identity. You're a woman who lifts.
