The Macro Cheat Sheet for Busy Women

Protein, carbs, and fats — the only three nutrition rules you need. Built for women who don't have time to weigh every chicken breast.

Kendra's promise: You don't need to track every macro forever. You just need to know what's in your plate, what's missing, and how to fix it in 60 seconds.

The 3 Targets (Daily)

MacroTarget for active women 130-170 lbsWhy
Protein110-140gBuilds muscle, controls appetite, hardest to overeat
Carbs150-220gEnergy for workouts, fuels brain, supports recovery
Fat50-70gHormones, satiety, vitamin absorption

Bodyweight outside 130-170? Add or subtract ~5g protein per 10 lbs. This is a starting point, not a prescription.

Protein Sources (30g portions)

Cooked weight

  • Chicken breast: 4 oz
  • Lean beef: 4 oz
  • Salmon: 5 oz
  • Shrimp: 6 oz
  • Tofu (firm): 7 oz

Off the shelf

  • 3 large eggs + 1 oz cheese: 27g
  • Greek yogurt (1 cup, 2%): 23g
  • Cottage cheese (1 cup): 24g
  • Protein shake (1 scoop + milk): 28g
  • Canned tuna (1 can): 25g

The 60-Second Plate Check

Look at your plate. Ask three questions:

  1. Is a fist of protein on it? If no, add one.
  2. Is there color (vegetables or fruit)? If no, add one.
  3. Are the carbs from real food, not packets? Sweet potato, rice, oats, fruit, beans — yes. Cereal, chips, crackers — rarely.

The "Eat Out" Framework

  • Start with protein. Pick the protein dish first. Steak, salmon, chicken, eggs.
  • Two sides: one vegetable, one starch. No fries-only nights.
  • Sauce on the side. Saves 200-400 calories effortlessly.
  • One drink rule. One alcoholic drink at dinner, then water. Liver thanks you.
