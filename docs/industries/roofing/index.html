Roofing FAQ

Questions roofing companies ask us first.

I get most of my roofing jobs from storm canvassing and referrals. Do I need a website?

Canvassing gets you in the door — but every homeowner you hand a card to Googles your company before they call back. A missing or weak site means that warm lead goes cold fast. A real website confirms your licensing, shows your project gallery, and captures the homeowners who search before you ever knock.

What about Angi, Thumbtack and HomeAdvisor for roofing leads?

Those platforms sell the same lead to multiple roofers and charge per contact. Your own website is a permanent asset — it earns leads directly, builds your brand with a gallery and reviews, and the cost stays flat at $50/mo no matter how many jobs it books.

I have a Facebook page. Isn't that enough for my roofing business?

A homeowner with storm damage searches Google — not Facebook. Facebook pages rarely rank for "roofing company near me," can't showcase a project gallery effectively, and don't give homeowners the trust signals — license, insurance, reviews — they need before handing someone $10,000.

Will my roofing website actually show up on Google?

Every site we build ships SEO-ready: proper title tags, local-business schema, service-area pages, and fast mobile load — the foundations Google rewards. Paired with your Google Business Profile, you get a real shot at the local map pack and the "roofing contractor near me" results.
