Mindset Reset — 5 Daily Habits

Kendra teaches these five habits to every client in week one. They take less than 10 minutes total per day. They compound into real consistency — even on the hard days.

Why mindset is the leverage point: The workout matters. The food matters. But the woman who keeps showing up after week three is the one who decided who she is — not just what she's doing.

Habit 1 · The 60-Second Morning Question

Before phone. Before coffee. Sit on the edge of the bed and ask:

"What's the one thing I want to be proud of tonight?"

Write it on a sticky note. One thing. That's the day's mission.

Habit 2 · Move Before You Think

Five minutes of movement before you open email or social media. Walk around the block, stretch for five minutes, or do 20 air squats. The body wakes up the mind — not the other way around.

Habit 3 · The Identity Sentence

Write this on the same sticky note as Habit 1:

"I am a woman who __________."

Fill in the blank with something already true. I am a woman who finishes what she starts. I am a woman who shows up. I am a woman who lifts. Read it out loud. Identity drives behavior, not the other way around.

Habit 4 · The Two-Minute Reset

Set a phone alarm for 2 PM every day. When it goes off:

  • 10 deep breaths (4 in, 6 out)
  • Stand up and roll shoulders 5 times each direction
  • Drink a full glass of water

That's it. Two minutes. The 2 PM slump is real; this is the cheapest fix.

Habit 5 · The Evening Replay

Before bed, three lines in a notebook:

  1. What went well today? (one thing, however small)
  2. Where did I show up? (one moment, no matter how minor)
  3. What does tomorrow's me need from tonight's me? (water by the bed? clothes laid out? phone in the kitchen?)

Three lines. Sixty seconds. Done.

The Compound Effect

None of these habits change anything in a day. All five together, done for 6 weeks, change who you are. Not what you do — who you are. And once that shifts, the workouts and the food stop feeling like effort.
