The 7-Day Kickstart Guide

The exact starter protocol Kendra Washington gives every new 1:1 client. Seven days of workouts, a sample meal plan, and the simple rules that make week one feel doable.

How to use this guide: Read it once start to finish. Pick a start date in the next 7 days. Tell one person you trust. Then begin. You don't need to be perfect — you just need to start.

The 3 Starter Rules

  1. Protein first. Aim for 30g of protein at each main meal. Eggs, Greek yogurt, chicken, fish, cottage cheese, lean beef, tofu — any of these work.
  2. Move every day. Four strength sessions, three walks. Walks count. A walk after dinner is not optional — it's the easiest way to control blood sugar and stress.
  3. Sleep is training. 7 hours minimum. Phone out of the bedroom. No exceptions for week one.

The 7-Day Training Plan

Day 1 · Lower body strength
Squats 3×10, Romanian deadlifts 3×10, glute bridges 3×12, walking lunges 2×10 each leg. 25 minutes.
Day 2 · Walk + mobility
30-minute walk outside. 10 minutes of hip openers, shoulder rolls, and cat-cow.
Day 3 · Upper body strength
Dumbbell rows 3×10, push-ups (knees ok) 3×8-12, shoulder press 3×10, bicep curls 2×12. 25 minutes.
Day 4 · Active rest
30-minute walk. Long shower. Stretch hamstrings and chest for 5 minutes.
Day 5 · Full body strength
Goblet squats 3×10, dumbbell deadlifts 3×10, dumbbell press 3×10, plank 3×30 sec. 30 minutes.
Day 6 · Long walk + reflect
45-60 minute walk. Phone on do-not-disturb. Notice what's changed.
Day 7 · Rest & plan
Real rest day. Plan groceries for next week. Write down what you learned about your body in 7 days.

What to Track

  • Did I move today? (Yes/No)
  • Did I hit my protein at each meal? (Yes/No)
  • Sleep hours. (Number)
  • Energy 1-10. (Number)

That's it. Don't weigh yourself this week. Pay attention to energy, mood, and how clothes fit.
